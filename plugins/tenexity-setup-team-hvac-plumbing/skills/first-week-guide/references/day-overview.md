# First Week Guide: Day overview

Quick reference for what each day covers, what it produces, and what it depends on.

## Day 1: A vendor email in your voice

- Principle: Push back and iterate (Cookbook Principle 3)
- Produces: A real vendor email — price pushback, rebate dispute, allocation complaint, or QBR prep
- Requires: About Me files filled in (voice-profile and writing-rules must be real content)
- Connectors needed: None
- Key moment: The first iteration where Claude's draft sounds nothing like the user — then iterating to where it does
- Save location: `WORK AREAS/Procurement-Vendors/[vendor]-correspondence-project/outputs/`

## Day 2: AR aging triage

- Principle: Describe the destination, not the journey (Cookbook Principle 1)
- Produces: A prioritised AR action list with five draft collection emails for Tier 1 accounts
- Requires: AR aging export from ERP, pasted into chat
- Connectors needed: None (Gmail optional to save emails as drafts)
- Key moment: Seeing pattern flags (branch-concentrated aging, rep-concentrated aging, segment-concentrated aging) the user didn't spot
- Save location: `WORK AREAS/Finance/[quarter]-ar-triage-project/outputs/`

## Day 3: A real customer quote

- Principle: Front-load what Claude can't guess (Cookbook Principle 2)
- Produces: A filled-in customer quote using the customer-quote-template, ready for price verification and send
- Requires: A real RFQ or project bid on the user's desk
- Connectors needed: None
- Key moment: Claude asks for specific ERP data rather than inventing prices — proves the "no invented numbers" rule in practice
- Save location: `WORK AREAS/Sales-Ops/[customer]-[job]-quote-project/outputs/`

## Day 4: Monthly dead-stock / slow-mover analysis

- Principle: Make Claude think harder (Cookbook Principle 4) — Confidence Check move
- Produces: A dead-stock analysis with every SKU classified into 5 buckets with recommended action + a Confidence Check of assumptions
- Requires: Dead-stock or slow-mover ERP export
- Connectors needed: None
- Key moment: The cash-freed-up summary at the end. Most distributors underestimate how much capital is sitting in dead stock until this table runs
- Save location: `WORK AREAS/Inventory-Ops/[month]-dead-stock-project/outputs/`

## Day 5: Top-account QBR prep

- Principle: Build systems, not one-offs (Cookbook Principle 5)
- Produces: A one-page QBR for the meeting + a 24-hour follow-up email draft
- Requires: A top account with a QBR scheduled or overdue, plus YTD/prior-year purchase data
- Connectors needed: None (Gmail helps for follow-up email draft)
- Key moment: The five-day close — the user sees the pattern behind all five days and realises it scales
- Save location: `WORK AREAS/Customer-Accounts/[account]-qbr-project/outputs/`

## Cookbook cross-references

Each day maps to a Cookbook principle. When the principle comes up naturally, mention the Cookbook reference:

- "Your Cookbook calls this 'Describe the destination' — Principle 1"
- "That's 'Front-load what Claude can't guess' — Principle 2 in your Cookbook"
- "Your Cookbook calls this 'Push back and iterate' — Principle 3"
- "That's from the 'Make Claude think harder' section — Principle 4 in your Cookbook"
- "This is 'Build systems, not one-offs' — Principle 5 in your Cookbook"

Don't force every reference. Mention the Cookbook when the principle is being demonstrated and the reference would help the user find it again later.

## On the Day 5 Tenexity close

Day 5 is the only place in the First Week Guide with a direct Tenexity CTA. It lands after the user has:
- Received five pieces of real output they'll use
- Seen the "data-in, data-out" pattern five times
- Been reminded that manual monthly cadences compound into real hours

The pitch is earned at that moment, not before. Keep it to the script in the SKILL.md. Don't add Tenexity mentions on Days 1-4 beyond what's already in the About Me files.
